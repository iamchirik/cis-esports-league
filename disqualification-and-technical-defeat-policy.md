1.1. In the event that a participant in the Competition commits a violation not expressly provided for in the Rules of Electronic Sports, these Regulations and the Technical Rules, the organizer of the Competition and the State Judicial Committee have the right to apply to such participant one of the sport sanctions provided for in the rules of electronic sports, guided by the analogy of law and the principles of good faith, reasonableness and fairness.

1.2. For a delay in the start or continuation of a match of more than 10 minutes, a participant may be awarded a technical defeat in the match.

1.3. Excessive messages (flooding) in the in-game "chat" may result in the application of sport sanctions to the offender, ranging from a warning to disqualification, depending on the number and frequency of messages.

1.4. Sabotaging matches may result in the application of sporting sanctions to the offender, including disqualification.

1.5. It is prohibited to start a game with an incomplete team roster. Violating this rule may result in a technical defeat for the team.

1.6. Participants who have ever been banned by a video game publisher and/or manufacturer, or who have been found guilty of cheating, including using prohibited software, or who have a reputation that will negatively impact the public perception of the Competition, may be disqualified from participating or disqualified without the right to appeal.

1.7. The procedure for appealing applied sporting sanctions may also be determined in other regulatory documents of the Organizer.
